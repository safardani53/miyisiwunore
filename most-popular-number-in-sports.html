13 Most Popular Number In Sports [2023 Update]

Famous athletes are well remembered for their accomplishments and the jersey they wore. The jersey has the players’ names written on it and the number that represents the player, making them the most popular number in sports.

The fans remember some of the most iconic players in sports history with the number they wore. Similarly, young aspiring players also wear the jersey number of their icons.

But there also is the fact that the players can wear more than one number during their career. The players are associated with the number they are most famous with their fans at such times.

So today, we are looking at some of the most popular numbers in sports and the iconic players who wore the iconic numbers.

Now without further ado, let us look at some of the most popular numbers the players wore.

13 Most Popular Number In Sports

The list has been compiled with the help of various internet sources like BleacherReport, Yardbarker, etc. But before diving into the details, let us look at the list below.

13. Wilt Chamberlain (13)

Let us start our list of the most popular numbers in sports with the number 13. Now number 13 may be considered unlucky by many, but there could not be any lucky number for basketball player Wilt Chamberlain.

Chamberlain is regarded as one of the greatest players in history. Throughout his playing career, he played in the number 13 jersey.

During his career, Chamberlain played for teams like the Harlem Globetrotters, Golden State Warriors, Los Angeles Lakers, and Philadelphia 76ers. He accumulated some of the most impressive wins and awards in his career.

After his professional career, all four franchises retired his number 13. In 1978, Chamberlain was enshrined in the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame.

12. Derek Jeter (2)

On our number 12, we have the former professional baseball player Derek Jeter and his number 2 jersey as the most popular number in sports. He spent the entire 20 years of his MLB career playing for the New York Yankees.

In his younger days, Jeter wanted to wear the number 13, but he played in number 2 after his debut in MLB in 1995.

He is often considered one of the primary contributors to the Yankees’ success in the league during the late 1990s and early 2000s. He earned nicknames like “Captain Clutch” and “Mr. November for his outstanding play.”

Jeter is one of the best players to have worn the jersey number 2. After the 2014 season, Jeter retired from his professional baseball career.

Later in 2017, the New York Yankees also retired his number 2 jersey in his honor. In 2020, Jeter became the Baseball Hall of Famer.

9. Magic Johnson (32)

Nicknamed “The Iron Man,” we have a former baseball shortstop and third baseman Cal Ripken Jr on our number 8 of the most popular number in sports.

7. Richard Petty (43)

The next player is not famous for his jersey number but for driving the No. 43 Plymouth/Pontiac. He is a former second-generation stock car racing driver nicknamed “The King” Richard Petty.

Petty raced from 1958 to 1992 in the former NASCAR Grand National and Winston Cup Series (now NASCAR Cup Series.) He was one of the first stars of NASCAR.

Petty is one of the most respected motorsports figures and most accomplished drivers in the sport’s history. Similarly, he was also the first driver to win the Cup Series championship seven times.

He has raced more than 2,000 NASCAR Cup Series races, among which he was behind the wheels of the No. 43 for 1,125 races.

Among other drivers who drove the No. 43, Petty is the only player who dominated the race with it. In 2010, he was inducted into the inaugural class of the NASCAR Hall of Fame.

6. Brett Favre (4)

Our number 6 of the most popular number in sports goes to Brett Favre. The former quarterback played 20 seasons in NFL.

Favre made his debut in NFL in 1991 with the Atlanta Falcon. The Falcon drafted him in the second round of the 1991 NFL draft, and he played with the number 4 jersey.

In his 20 years career, he primarily played for the Green Bay Packers. He also played with the New York Jets and Minnesota Vikings. Fave played in the number 4 jersey throughout his career.

Likewise, he has an impressive list of career highlight wins and awards. He was the NFL leader in passing yards, passing touchdowns, and quarterback wins in his retirement.

The Packers retired number 4 after Favre’s retirement. He was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 2016.

5. Wayne Gretzky (99)

The number 99 is one of the most recognizable numbers in hockey history. The number is synonymous with the former Canadian ice hockey player Wayne Gretzky.

Gretzky played 20 seasons in NHL and then transitioned to head coach. One of the greatest hockey players, the player is nicknamed “The Great One.”

Gretzky made many records during his career. In NHL history, he is the leading goal, assist producer, and point scorer. When he retired in 1999, he had held excellent 61 NHL records.

The number 99 that Greyzky wore is so iconic that there is even a trophy named after it called the “Wayne Gretzky 99 Award.”

He immediately became the Hockey Hall of Famer after retiring. In 2000, the Great One’s number 99 was also retired league-wide.

The former football quarterback Peyton Manning’s iconic number 18 is our fourth most famous number in sports. He is considered one of the greatest quarterbacks of all time.

3. Babe Ruth (3)

Babe Ruth is a legendary figure in baseball. His number 3 is popular among every baseball fan in the world. He is considered to be the greatest baseball player of all time.

During his professional career, Ruth also played for the New York Yankees and Boston Braves. His career in MLB spanned 22 years, and he primarily played with the Yankees.

The Yankees retired Ruth’s iconic number 3 after his retirement. In 1936, he was inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame. He passed away at age 53 on August 16, 1948.

2. Jackie Robinson (42)

Our number 2 of the most popular number in sports goes to the first African American player, Jackie Robinson, number 42. The player is a legendary figure and a trailblazer who broke the color barrier in the sport.

Robinson made his MLB debut in 1947 with the Brooklyn Dodger. The Dodgers signing him marked an end of racial segregation in baseball. Therefore, his number 42 carries a massive significance in baseball history.

His career in MLB spanned ten years. Robinson became All-Star, World Series champion, NL Most Valuable Player six times, etc. He was the first black player to achieve the honor.

After his retirement, all MLB teams retired the number 42. It shows that the number 42 signifies a piece of history in baseball. The legend became the Baseball Hall of Famer in 1962.

Jackie Robinson passed away on October 24, 1972, at age 53.

1. Michael Jordan (23)

We bet you see this coming. Our most famous number in sports is the former NBA player Michael Jordan’s number 23. He is considered the greatest basketball player and a cultural icon.

Jordan played an integral part in popularizing NBA worldwide in the 1980s and 1990s. He made his debut in the NBA in 1984 with Chicago Bulls and spent 15 seasons playing in the NBA.

Although he played with number 23 at the beginning of his career, Jordan later changed into number 42. However, the number 23 became synonymous with him and his image.

The face of the NBA, Jordan retired after the 2003 season. With his retirement, the Chicago Bulls and Miami Heat retired the number.

There could be no denying that the number 23 will forever be the most iconic in the history of the NBA.
