Exact date to apply for one off relief payment of $500 from $15million pot is extended - get lump su

STRUGGLING Americans have until the end of the month to apply for a one-time relief payment worth $500.

The Chicago Resiliency Fund 2.0 program will now shut on December 31 rather than December 9 as was originally reported.

Officials will provide up to 25,500 residents with a one-time check that is being funded from a $14.68million pot.

The program is aimed at “providing cash assistance to Chicagoans who may have been left out of COVID-19 stimulus."

Applicants have to submit an online form by the end of the month and candidates will be randomly selected in a lottery draw.

Chicagoans must be over the age of 18 and have an income that is at or below 300 per cent of the Federal Poverty Level.

For a household of one, the income threshold is $40,770.

Americans must also meet one of the following criteria:

  • They filed taxes in 2019 and claimed a dependent aged 17 or older
  • They are an undocumented resident
  • Or, they are a domestic worker.

Officials have warned that only one application per household can be submitted as duplicates will be rejected.

If selected, candidates will have to pass eligibility checks designed to prevent fraud or any other criminal activities.

The money will either be uploaded onto a prepaid debit card or sent via bank transfer.

Officials have said that they hope to send out all payments by December 2023 at the latest.

Meanwhile, struggling Americans in Cleveland can apply for a winter crisis scheme that provides up to $800 in utility assistance.

This one-time payment will provide relief to households that have been disconnected, threatened with disconnection, or have less than 25 percent supply of bulk fuel in their tank to maintain service.

To qualify for the assistance, residents must have a gross income at or below 175 percent of the federal poverty guidelines.

The money will then be applied to the resident's utility bill or heating system repair costs.

The US Sun has outlined what Americans will need to bring with them when they apply for the assistance.

Cities across the country have introduced guaranteed income schemes in a bid to help cash-strapped citizens.

More than 170 people in Alexandria, Virginia will start pocketing monthly $500 payments for two years from January.

The funds are being given to struggling residents as part of the city’s ARISE program.

City of Alexandria councilmember Canek Aguirre told 7News: “As it’s titled, ARISE, we are trying to lift people up.”

Officials revealed that approximately 4,000 applications were submitted in total.

And, 3,000 people in Madison, Wisconsin applied to receive monthly $500 payments for 12 months.
