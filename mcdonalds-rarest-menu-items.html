I tried McDonalds rarest menu items - all of them were amazing, but the cheesy bites were best of

A FOODIE tucked into some of McDonald’s rarest menu items  and gave his verdict on each.

TikTok user DrHobs, whose profile is @ghobs, said he was excited to see what they tasted like, as he ordered the likes of Rice & Chicken and cheese bites – available in different countries around the world. 

First up, DrHobs sampled the Rice & Chicken that came in a large bowl. 

He took a large spoonful of the rice and couldn’t believe how amazing it tasted.

Giving it a huge seal of approval, the social media star told his 790,000 plus followers that the rice was “insane” and “incredible.”

He also went on to score it a whopping nine out of ten.

Next, DrHobs went for a simple crispy chicken breast, which managed to exceed even the first dish.

He said it was so “juicy” and delicious, and gave it a strong 9.5 out of 10.

The penultimate menu item was the fast food chain’s Camembert Bites, only available in the UK over the festive period, but in different countries all year round.

Each box contains three of the cheese snacks and is covered in a tasty breaded coating.

As soon as he opened the container to see what was inside, he exclaimed: “Oh my God.”

And things just got better from there, as he bit into one of the bites to see what he thought.

The foodie looked over the moon, as he asked: “Is this heaven?”

He then gave it a perfect 10 out of 10, saying it couldn’t be improved upon.

And last, but by no means least, Dr Hobs gave his followers a look at 3-Mushroom Brioche Burger.

As it says in the name, a soft but sweet brioche bun is filled with a mushroom and cheese filling.

The result? Dr Hobs described the burger’s flavour as “insanity” and it got a good 8.5. 

Unsurprisingly, it wasn’t long before Dr Hobs’ followers told him that they were jealous of his McDonald’s feast.

One commented: “We don’t have those things in our McDonald’s here in Belgium. Why? It looks so yummy!”

As another added: “I want camembert bites please.”
