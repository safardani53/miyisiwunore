Lizzo fans go wild as ageless Tyson Beckford, 51, makes out with singer in new video 26 years after

LIZZO fans are going wild as the ageless Tyson Beckford, 51, made out with the pop singer in her new video.

Tyson's appearance in Lizzo's 2 Be Loved (Am I Ready) came 26 years after the model wow-ed fans as he swam shirtless through a pool in a Toni Braxton music video for her song Un-Break My Heart.

Lizzo's latest music video follows the star posing as a runaway bride as she makes out with the hunky model after he rides in on a motorcycle.

By kissing the model on a motorbike, Lizzo gave a nod to Tyson's appearance in Britney Spears' Toxic where the star rode on the back of a motorcycle with the model in the 2004 music video.

Toxic came after Tyson made an appearance in Un-Break My Heart alongside Toni in 1996 - also with an iconic motorcycle moment.

Tyson's fans flocked to Twitter to gush over his hot and steamy moment with Lizzo in her new video.

LIZZO DISS-O

Lizzo has been keeping other celebrities buzzing over her music as well.

Recently, Whoopi Goldberg, 66, snubbed the singer on The View.

Whoopie targeted Lizzo after she discussed her opinion on monogamy in a recent interview.

The TV personality opened up the conversation, saying: "Three-time Grammy winner Lizzo has been with her boyfriend for about a year and said in a recent interview that she thinks people over-complicate their relationships."

She prompted: "Take a look," as cameras cut to a clip from Lizzo's recent chat.

The singer stated her position on being monogamous and told fans: "I think a love relationship that's not monogamous has no rules.

"And I think people who do poly and all that stuff, there's still rules. It's like 'well you can see this person but you can't see this person.'"

She continued: "I don't want any rules. I'm not worried about nobody else sexually, romantically, or emotionally.

"I'm just worried about you. And I'm just thinking about you. And I think that's what we have which is the most beautiful, pure thing ever," Lizzo gushed.

But Whoopi was not impressed, and as the cameras switched back to the live taping, she smirked in silence to mock the vocalist's opinion as the other hosts laughed in response.

"I feel like I'm the wrong person to be talking about any of this, you know," the comedian joked as the crowd erupted in laughter.
