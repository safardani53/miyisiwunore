Big Brother Season 25 LIVE Jared Fields slammed by Jag's family in scathing statement after usin

BIG Brother has avoided addressing the newest incident involving the Week 5 Head of Household, Jared Fields, and a houseguest's family is not letting it slide.

While the CBS series continues to disregard Jared's use of a slur during Labor Day weekend, Jag Bains' family issued a strong statement through social media.

Jared was not thrilled with America Lopez, believing she was deliberately losing competitions, and viewers with access to live feeds over the weekend witnessed a heated debate between Cory Wurtenberger and Jared about America, where Jared called her the "r-word" slur.

The scene was not shown on the most recent Big Brother program, but a video of Jared apologizing for the slur has been circulating since the Week 5 veto battle.

The family of Season 25 Houseguest Jag Bains responded to CBS and Big Brother's lack of action, writing on Instagram, in part: "Such language is entirely unacceptable, particularly on a prominent platform like Big Brother."

The statement issued by Jag's family was supported by the Special Olympics official account, which posted its own statement in response to the post, writing: "Special Olympics stands behind Jag Bains and his family in their condemnation of the use of the r-word by a contestant on CBS’s Big Brother."

Meanwhile, Red Utley was evicted from the Big Brother Sunday house by a vote of 8-2 and Cameron was crowned the next Head of Household.

  • Cooking directions for slop

    To make your own slop, first bring 12 cups of water to a boil and add the steel-cut oats.

    Cook for about 25-30 minutes while stirring constantly to stop the oats from getting stuck to the pan.

    Once the oats are cooked, the other ingredients are simply added and combined.

  • Ingredients you need to make slop

    To make the slop at home, you will need 3.5 cups of uncooked steel-cut oats.

    The recipe also requires 3/4 cup, plus two tablespoons of unflavored whey protein powder.

    A key ingredient is also the half cup of unflavored soy protein.

    In order to make sure the contestants get their daily nutrients, the recipe also asks for four teaspoons of vitamin and mineral powder.

  • Big Brother Slop, explained

    Slop is a meal that contestants on Big Brother are made to eat.

    Only contestants who are being punished are made to eat the basic food.

    The “Have-Nots” on the TV show are given slop for all three meals in a day.

    It is a bland meal that consists of very few ingredients, including steel-cut oats, whey protein, soy protein, vitamin powder, and mineral powder.

  • More on Sound Stage 18

    Brooklyn Nine-Nine, The Talk, Will & Grace, and That 70’s Show were also filmed at Radford.

    Year after year, Sound Stage 18 is redesigned to fit the theme of that season’s BB USA.

    For season 24 of Big Brother, the format was festival-themed and required the House Guests to participate in challenges that involved a music festival, a renaissance festival, and so forth.

    Season 23 featured a beach house theme and nautical challenges were a part of the competition.

  • All about the Big Brother house

    The Big Brother USA house is actually Sound Stage 18 in the CBS Studios Center.

    Also known as the Radford Studio Center, BB USA’s filming studio is located in the Studio City district of Los Angeles.

    In May 1928, Radford Studio Center was founded by silent film producer Mack Sennett.

    In addition to BB USA, the studio has served as a production location for several other hit shows.

  • How the Pressure Cooker works

    The Pressure Cooker is an iconic Big Brother endurance competition.

    During the Pressure Cooker competition, the houseguests are all put in a glass box where they each have to hold down a button.

    The last person to take their finger off the buzzer wins.

  • You are cooked

    The longest endurance competition in Big Brother history took 14 hours and 37 minutes.

    The historic competition, also known as the Pressure Cooker, took place during season six, and contestants were required to hold down a button to remain in the game.

    The Pressure Cooker competition returned earlier this season for the first time since season six.

  • All about ZingBot

  • Jag Bains makes history

    Jag Bains is the first Sikh houseguest to join Big Brother.

    This isn’t something he takes lightly, telling Parade that he’s proud to represent his community.

    “Representing my Sikh community is so important to me,” he said. “I wear a turban. This is how I represent myself every day. So to come to the show, be who I am, represent not only myself, but my family, my people, my community, it means the world to me.”

    He echoed the sentiment on Instagram, writing: “As the first-ever Sikh on the show, I feel truly honored, humbled, and blessed.  I’m deeply grateful for this opportunity to represent my community and share my story with the world.”

  • Cast paychecks revealed

    Big Brother houseguests do get paid for appearing on the show.

    The amount they earn at the end of the show depends on how long they stay in the game.

    Houseguests get paid $1k each week they stay in the house, according to Reel Rundown – so the longer they survive, the more each contestant gets paid.

  • Grand prize revealed

    The winner of the top two prizes does not receive the weekly stipend but instead is awarded their allotted sum of money.

    The grand prize winner receives $750k, the runner-up gets $75k, and America’s favorite player gets $50k.

    There are other incentives during the houseguests’ stay as well to boost their income with the grand prize representing the golden jackpot.
