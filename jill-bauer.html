Jill Bauer Biography

Women Of All Seasons Jill Bauer: Her Career Details

Jill Bauer worked as a research assistant for Esquire magazine, where she stayed until 1993. The same year she published her first book non-fiction humor  which was published on April 1, 1993. Two years later she launched magazine in September 1995. She also worked as the managing editor for SmarKid magazine until September 1997. 

Furthermore, Jill worked as an editor and journalist at the Miami Herald, Miami Magazine, The New York Times and The Dallas Morning News. Then she started to work at the University of Miami magazine from 2006. She worked there for three years.

Bauer along with Ronna Gradus left their job at Miami Herald and started to work on the documentary projects. They both debuted as a director and producer for the 2012 documentary .  Her documentary won Best Film by a First-Time Director at Traverse City Film Festival. 

The Duo work together again for Hot Girls Wanted which was released in 2015. From their work, they were nominated for Primetime Emmy Awards for Exceptional Merits in Documentary Filmmaking.

In 2017, Jill's television series documentary Hot Girls Wanter: Turned On was released.
