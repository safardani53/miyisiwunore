Hacked Deliveroo accounts sold on Dark Web for under 5 letting crooks go on 'Nandos and cider'

DELIVEROO account holders are being warned that hacked accounts are being sold on the dark web for as little as £5.

Users are advised to change their passwords and check their account details for any purchases they didn't make if they think they could have been affected.

According to Forbes, a "significant number of customers" have been affected.

Witnesses are said to be noticing strange orders for bizarre food and alcohol being charged to their accounts, including one that amounted to a £450 fee.

Forbes claims to have had two staff members affected by the Deliveroo hacking over the past two months and tweets about being hacked appear on Twitter every few days.

A Deliveroo spokesperson said: “Deliveroo takes online security extremely seriously and has robust measures both to protect our systems and members of the public who have had their passwords compromised outside of Deliveroo."

They added: "Sadly cyber criminals rely on the fact that people reuse the same passwords on multiple online services and use data breaches on other sites to try gain access to Deliveroo accounts.

"There has been no breach of Deliveroo’s internal systems.

“We continuously roll out measures to combat fraudsters and have introduced further security this year to help protect customers from fraudulent charges when their accounts have been accessed.

"These steps have made a huge difference and we will continue to actively support customers to strengthen their online security by using different passwords online."

Deliveroo has said that in most hacking cases the account holder will receive a full refund from Deliveroo or their bank.

Back in 2016, hundreds of Deliveroo customers reported having their accounts hacked with fraudsters stealing money from their bank accounts.

Many of the customers affected took to Twitter to complain about food orders - some worth hundreds of pounds - which were delivered to addresses that they didn't recognise.
