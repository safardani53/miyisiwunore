What is 239 pounds in stone, how much does Donald Trump weigh and what is the 'Girther' Twitter move

AS President Donald Trump's health assessment results were released in January 2018 he was found to be in good health, if a little overweight.

But how much does the 45th president actually weigh and what does that mean?

What is 239 pounds in stone and how much does Donald Trump weigh?

The President weighs 17.07 stone, and at 6ft 3inches has a BMI of 29.

This means he is classed as overweight - but some people questioned if this was really what Trump weighed.

Suspicious people on social media immediately began to compare his height and weight to sportsmen with similar figures.

Tom Brady, who is 6ft 4inches, weighs 225lbs and looks to be of a slimmer frame to the president.

Dr Ronny Jackson, the White House physician, said Trump was in "excellent health" but advised him to improve his diet and exercise.

In more obscure figures Trump weighs the same as about 23.9 cats, 34 average sized newborn babies or almost three goats.

What is the 'Girther' Twitter movement?

The "Girther" movement was sparked from questions arising over the president's weight.

It references Trump's calls for Barack Obama to release his birth certificate, which was dubbed the birther movement.

Twitter users posted comments containing jibes about Trump's weight and suggesting he weighs more than the official figure - each post ended with "#girther".

Calls to see Trump's "Girth Certificate" were made as Twitter remained unconvinced that their president was in "excellent health".

The Guardians of the Galaxy director even offered to give $100,000 to Trump's favourite charity if he publicly stepped on scales to prove his weight.

What measuring system do we use in the UK?

The UK uses a pound and stones system - but the US sticks with pounds, rather than working out how many stones make up the pounds.

There are 14 pounds in a stone, so if something is 15 pounds, we would say it is 1 stone 1.

The stone is a unit of measure equal to 6.3503 kilograms.

So President Trump weighs 108.5kg.

What other measuring units do we use?

In the UK we use both the metric and imperial system for measuring things.

The metric system includes the kilogram, millilitre and metric tonne.

The imperial system uses pounds and ounces - often used for baking measurements.
