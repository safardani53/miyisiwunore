DAVID BECKHAM'S Inter Miami have released their first-ever away kit... but fans are NOT happy with the £104 pink and black design.

The former England captain's MLS side finally kick-off next month in Los Angeles even though their squad and stadium are still being built.

Becks 44, has already sorted a name, a kit and crest and a derelict stadium 25 miles away from Miami airport as a two-year temporary home.

But the former Manchester United star's unveiling of Inter's inaugural away strip failed to hit the target as fans were left underwhelmed by the effort.

The kit, dubbed the 'RosaNegra jersey', reverts the colours of the club's home shirt featuring a black template.

The strip is then complimented by flashes of pink logos and brandings 'to add an unmistakable mark of South Florida', with the club mantra “Freedom to Dream” scrawled on the inside collar.

The kit is currently able to pre-order for a whopping £104 but fans may choose to save their money after sharing their lack of enthusiasm at the release.

One tweeted: "I generally like black shirts but this is so disappointing", to which another replied: "It's lazy, honestly. Couldn't agree more."

Twitter user Palmetto Palace wrote: "I don’t think you can in good conscience even call that a kit."

However, some fans were impressed by the inaugural design, with one more adding: "I see people complaining, I’m not sure why. I love it."

Earlier this week, Beckham had given fans a sneak peak inside Inter Miami's new PINK stadium after admitting that his "dream" was becoming a reality.

Fans were left fearing the worst after pictures last month showed the club's temporary home looking like a construction site.

But Beckham proved the £50million project is close to completion with his latest Instagram snaps.
