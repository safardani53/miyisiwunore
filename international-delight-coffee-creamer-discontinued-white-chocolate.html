International Delight confirms popular coffee creamer is no longer being made and fans say it

INTERNATIONAL Delight has confirmed that a fan-favorite flavor has been axed from the shelves.

The coffee creamer company is one of the most prominent brands seen in grocery stores – competing with Nestle-owned Coffee-Mate.

While fans often fall in love with new flavors – they could be discontinued at any point.

For example, one Twitter user recently asked International Delight about the availability of the white chocolate macadamia flavor.

The coffee creamer brand in response said: "Sadly, it is no longer being made. We're so sorry!"

The fan replied and said that the flavor was the "best."

Another in the same thread tweeted a few weary face emojis in reaction to the news.

Some of the white chocolate macadamia flavor's ingredients consisted of natural flavors, carrageenan, artificial flavors, palm oil, and five grams of added sugar, according to the product details description on Kroger.

It sold previously in a 32-ounce bottle but the price was unclear.

OTHER ALTERNATIVES TO BUY

While none may hit the spot like the white chocolate macadamia variety, coffee creamer fans might like other options sold at the grocery store.

Most read in Money

In fact, Starbucks makes a white chocolate mocha creamer, which features seed oils, natural flavors and comes with five added sugars.

While prices can vary by location, a 28-ounce container sells at Walmart for $5.46.

However, customers only rate it 3.1 out of five stars.

Also, International Delight fans might enjoy a better deal on other flavors such as Hershey's chocolate caramel, southern butter pecan, and caramel macchiato.

They all sell in 32-ounce containers for just $3.77.

MORE DISCOTNUNIED COFFEE CREAMERS

But unfortunately, there is more bad news for coffee creamer fans.

International Delight has discontinued its Cold Stone Creamery flavor.

The creamer was both gluten and lactose-free, which made it available to a wide range of coffee drinkers.

When it comes to rival Coffee-Mate, the company confirmed that its cookies 'n cocoa flavor has been axed.

The Cookies 'N Cocoa creamer consisted of seed oils, natural and artificial flavors, and carrageenan.

Also, it had five grams of added sugar.

Previously, the creamer was sold in a 32-fluid-ounce container for $4.69 at Kroger.

Another Coffee-Mate causality is the Chocolate Raspberry flavor.

It was actually axed before 2012 – but fans have been begging for it to come back.

For more related stories, Gatorade has axed its blueberry pomegranate flavor.
