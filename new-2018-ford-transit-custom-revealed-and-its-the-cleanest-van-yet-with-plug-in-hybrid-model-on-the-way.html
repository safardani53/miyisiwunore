New 2018 Ford Transit Custom revealed - and it's the cleanest van yet with plug-in hybrid model on t

THE all-new Ford Transit has been revealed and it's promising to clean up its act with a new plug-in hybrid (PHEV) model.

The 2018 Transit Custom gets a bold new design, fresh interior and more efficient engines.

The Dagenham-built EcoBlue 2.0-litre diesel will be available from launch but by 2019 there will be a zero-emission PHEV on sale.

A PHEV Transit Custom will begin trials with fleets in London later this year.

The standard engines are available in 104bhp, 128bhp and 168bhp while a brand new ECOnetic variant will offer 49.6mpg and  148g/km of CO2.

That's possible thanks to the lower-powered engine being calibrated for economy with stop-start and low rolling resistance tyres to save fuel.

The one tonne commercial vehicle gets a new front end with three-bar grille and an interior carried over from the new Ford Fiesta.

It means it includes an eight-inch SYNC 3 infotainment system with voice commands compatible with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto.

There's 25-litres of storage space inside the cabin, too, with three bins for clipboard and smartphones, a generous glove box and a fold-out cupholder under the gear lever.

Intelligent Speed Limiter and Traffic Sign Recognition will be introduced alongside a blind spot warning system for the first time.

That's on top of the autonomous emergency braking system and lane keep assist already available on the existing Transit.

There are two roof heights, two wheelbase options with payload choices up to 1,450kg.

There's also a range-topping Sport series fitted with the 168bhp engine. It comes with the trademark twin bonnet stripes and exterior body kit.

Inside, there's partial leather trim and new embossed fabrics.

Prices haven't been confirmed for the Transit but expect it to start from around £25,000.

It will go on sale later this year with deliveries due to start early 2018.
