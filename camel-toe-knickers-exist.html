Camel toe knickers now exist and people don't know what to make of them

WHEN it comes to pants ideas, this one might just take the crown.

People are now buying 'camel toe knickers' which deliberately make it look like they've got a front wedgie, and we're feeling very confused.

That's right, the undies are padded with silicone at the front to create the illusion of a camel toe - and are being flogged on sites like Amazon and eBay.

The bonkers trend originated in Asia a decade ago, where women are thought to be using the knickers to accentuate their lady parts.

This is despite the fact that most women would curl up into a ball if anyone caught them with a camel toe.

You can buy them online for £8.59 - where people have, quite rightly, asked: "why is this a thing?" in the comments.

Others have asked why, even if someone does want a camel toe, they would bother buying the pants.

One person wrote: "Why would any female pay $10-$30 (£7.60-£22.80) for some underwear like this, when camel toe is free".

The answer may be because it isn't women buying, but trans ladies trying to hide a tell-tale bulge.

Which actually makes a lot more sense.
