Joan Jett is an American rock singer, songwriter, musician, musician, record producer, and actress. Jett is best known for her work as the lead vocalist for her band Joan Jett & the Blackhearts, as well as founding and performing with Runaways, who recorded and released the hit song "Cherry Bomb". For The Blackhearts, Jett known for his performance of the song "I Love Rock 'n Roll" topped the Billboard Hot 100 charts for seven weeks in 1982. Other notable Jett songs include " Bad Reputation "," Crimson and Clover "," You will touch me "," Daylight "," I hate myself for loving you "and" Dirty act ". Jett has a middle-low female voice. She has three albums certified platinum or gold. She is considered the queen of rock 'n' roll. In 2015, Joan Jett & the Blackhearts was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. Jett lives in Long Beach, New York and has resided in New York City since the late 1970s.
