Kim Kardashian's glam squad member reveals new 'official' job with A-list star as fans fear fall-out

A MEMBER of Kim Kardashian's glam squad has revealed a new career move with another A-list celeb.

Chris Appleton, Kim's hair stylist, has revealed that he will be a part of a popular daytime TV talk show.

Chris announced that he will be joining Drew Barrymore on The Drew Barrymore Show.

In a new photo posted to Instagram, Chris shared a picture with Drew to reveal the good news.

"Happy to be officially apart of the family @thedrewbarrymoreshow as lifestyle Contributor season 4," he wrote.

Kim has yet to congratulate Chris publically on his new job.

Other members of Kim's glam squad include Mario Dedivanovic, her makeup artist and founder of Makeup by Mario.

POSSIBLE FEUD?

Back in April, Kim and her daughter, North West, 9, attended The Daily Front Row's Fashion Los Angeles Awards in Beverly Hills where Chris won Hair Artist of the Year.

During Chris' speech, Kim stole the mic to say something "really quick" and it rubbed Kardashian fans the wrong way.

She interrupted his speech to say that she is "so happy" that Chris is in a relationship (with actor Lukas Gage).

MORE ON CHRIS

The hair stylist was born in Leicester, England.

The 6-foot-3 stud has a modeling contract with IMG Models, as well as an entertainment contract with WME.

He is also the global creative director of Color Wow hair products - which he often uses on his many celebrity clients.

Appleton's loyal customers include Ariana Grande, Katy Perry, Kate Moss, Cara Delevingne, and many more.

His work has been featured in top-tier magazines like Vogue, Harper’s Bazaar, Vanity Fair, and Marie Claire, as well as on the global runways of iconic fashion houses including Chanel, Fendi, Valentino, and Saint Laurent.

According to his bio by The Wall Group: "Impeccable attention to detail, paired with enviable creative vision, has made Appleton into one of the most in-demand hairstylists today."

In a conversation with his close friend, Kim, and Gay Times, Appleton explained that the most challenging aspect of his job "is the fact that you and I can like it, but then the rest of the world has an opinion. Or something might look great in real life but might not photograph the same.

“I think if nobody is discussing anything then you’re not doing the right thing – if people are just like ‘whatever’.”
