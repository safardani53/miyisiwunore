Hollywood's worst celebrity neighbors from Justin Bieber's 'war zone' to 'unbearably' noisy Blac Chy

MANY Hollywood celebrities have earned the reputation of being some of the worst neighbors someone could find themselves living beside.

Noisy house parties, late-night antics, and outright rude behavior have become commonplace among the most notable neighbors.

Though not alone in his communal mischief, popstar Justin Bieber, 29, became known as Hollywood's most notorious neighbor when he topped Zillow's annual list of most undesirable celebrity neighbors in 2016.

JUSTIN BIEBER

Justin may have cleaned up his behavior since then, but in the mid-2010s the Where Are U Now singer had garnered somewhat of a rapscallion reputation.

The Canada-born celebrity's worst offense was committed in 2014 when he pelted his neighbor's house with eggs.

He was charged with vandalism for the egging incident and ordered to pay $80,000 in restitution.

Justin's celebrity neighbors quickly grew discontent with the Peaches artist's constant partying and loud music.

The Good Place star Kristen Bell, 43, and Armchair Expert host Dax Shepard, 48, who lived next door to Justin in 2012 compared the situation to an active war zone.

“The music and the parties and the paparazzi, I mean, it’s like living in Lebanon now,” Dax explained when asked about the experience.

The Crowded Room actress Emmy Rossum, who was also neighbors with Justin, stated: "He has a ton of bodyguards there and they’re really scary," when gusting on SiriusXM’s Sway in the Morning in 2015.

She continued: "It was so surreal to me that I was like, 'I am not going to go ask for a cup of sugar over there.'"

That same year, Justin even admitted to needing to find a residence away from other people due to his noisy habits.

Realizing that his label of being the worst celebrity neighbor has some merit, he exclaimed: "I don’t want neighbors!

"I’m loud, I’m really loud. I’m gonna play my music really, really, really loud every night, I’m gonna play the drums. I’m gonna have a lot of people over, probably. And I can’t have neighbors, just because I don’t want to piss anyone off."

BLAC CHYNA

In 2017, Blac Chyna, 35, earned the rep of being a bad neighbor when the residents living inside her gated Calabasas, California community complained about activities in and around her home.

Chyna's "thug" friends were said to be climbing over the security walls of the neighborhood, instead of using the front gate, to hang out at the TV personality's home, as reported by TMZ.

Once inside, the reality TV star's acquaintances were also accused of making noise into the wee hours of the night.

Residents, some of whom considered Chyna's guests entering the neighborhood unchecked to be a "dangerous situation," are required to enter the community through the front gate — where there is a guard shack stationed.

TMZ reported that when police were called to the community for "an unrelated matter" the model's neighbors "flagged them down and ticked off a laundry list of complaints against Chyna."

The worst complaint was that the "thugs" were visiting their home.

The neighbors accused Chyna's friends of being "riffraff," and mentioned that the men did use the front gate but instead avoided security by jumping the wall.

The residents of the community, who are "affluent people" with diverse professions, reportedly told the cops they were afraid Chyna would bring property values down.

CHRIS BROWN

In 2016, Chris Brown, 34, scared the residents around his Tarzana, California home during an hours-long standoff with police.

Former NBA player Nick Young recorded a video of the debacle, which was eventually posted online, that captured some of the drama after cops showed up at the singer's home in response to a 911 call from a woman who said Chris was threatening her with a gun.

Nick, also known as Swaggy P, could be heard saying: "Chris Brown, you gotta move, man.

"Go on, get away from over here, please. You’re making the block hot, buddy."

He continued: "[They] got SWAT teams and helicopters flying over my head, [and] I’m thinking they’re after me. We only have about five or six black people over here anyway. … They’re going to take me [and] you.

"There’s really only two of us over here, man. And at this rate, you’re gonna make it one."

During the standoff, Brown reportedly pitched a duffel bag containing guns and drugs out of his window, TMZ reported.

He was later arrested on charges of assault with a deadly weapon before being released on $250,000 bail.

At the time of the incident, Chris had only lived in the neighborhood for about a year, but his antics had already garnered several complaints from his neighbors.

“I think he’s a piece of s**t,” Chris' neighbor Rudy Akbarian said.

“He’s rude, first of all. I’ll come walking out with my friends at 12 o’clock. I get home at 12 o’clock from work. He’ll be out here with his stupid little friends and their stupid little cars, revving their engines. Like dude, people are trying to sleep! This is a residential area!"

Another neighbor, singer Kay Cola, shared similar sentiments when she tweeted: "I even got into it with Chris because his loud a** used to wake up my daughter."

"He was rude as f**k and I had known him since he was a kid."

JENNIFER LOPEZ

In 2015, Jennifer Lopez's neighborly status was called into question after she allowed her neighbor's home to be used as a makeshift "holding area for knights and s**ting horses."

During an appearance on the podcast Allegedly with comedians Theo Von and Matthew Cole, America's Got Talent judge Howie Mandel, 67, dished on J. Lo's neighborhood antics.

He explained: "So I live in a cul-de-sac. I walk out one day and go to the bottom of the driveway and my cul-de-sac is filled with knights in shining armor on horses.

"It's medieval times."

The Love Don't Cost a Thing singer was throwing a medieval-themed party but failed to consider the disruption the animals and the costumed actors would cause by being allowed to roam the private street.

Howie complained: "They have jousting sticks. And all the horses are s**ting.

"There's so much horse s**t."

The former Deal or No Deal host continued: "There's knights and damsels. I go 'What the f**k? What is this?' And they go 'J. Lo is having a party and J. Lo ordered Medieval Times at her house."

GIGI HADID AND OTHERS

Model Gigi Hadid, 28, outed herself as "the worst neighbor" during a 2016 interview with Harper's Bazaar.

When discussing a night in, she disclosed: "I love to cook and watch a movie... I also love to sing Broadway show tunes at home, which makes me the worst neighbor but is a good stress reliever."

Gigi continued: "I love 'Popular' from Wicked.

"That's the only one I'll tell you about because I know at some point someone's going to ask me to do this, and that's the only one I'm willing to do publicly."

In 2010, cops were called to actor Katherine Heigl's home after an upset neighbor objected to her hot tub make-out session with her husband Josh Kelley.

Katherine, 44, told Ryan Seacrest while appearing on his radio show: "The other night, my husband and I, we were chillin' in the hot tub.

"[Our neighbor] starts screaming at us to go inside. I'm like, 'I've had it, I've had enough of this. It's ridiculous, it's harassment and I'm calling the police.'"

Photos of the Grey's Anatomy actress talking to cops while wearing a bikini later became known as #tubgate.

In 2016, it was revealed that the "no parking" signs in front of Madonna's $40 million Manhattan townhouse were fake and that the Queen of Pop had placed them there herself.

Neighbors claimed the traffic signs were not only illegal but also showed that the Vogue singer was "entitled."
